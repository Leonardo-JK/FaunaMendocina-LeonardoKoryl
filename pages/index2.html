Useful information

    Advice on behavior towards wildlife:

  1. IF POSSIBLE, VISIT THE ANIMALS IN THEIR NATURAL HABITAT
    If you plan the most comfortable option of visiting a sanctuary or zoo, make sure it is accredited and professionally managed.
  2. SEE ANIMALS BEHAVE NATURALLY
    Snake charmers and monkey circuses may be alluring, but what happens behind the scenes can horrify you. To avoid encouraging these practices, find a respected elephant sanctuary or take a jeep ride to admire the elephants in their natural habitat.
  3. IT IS PROHIBITED TO CARRY
    This instruction might disappoint you, but you have to understand the mistreatment that surrounds facilities that offer experiences of active participation or close contact with animals.
  4. SPEND MONEY IN A SUSTAINABLE WAY
    If you are really concerned about animal welfare and wildlife sustainability, consider hiring an expedition group rather than organizing each activity yourself.
  5. RESPECT ANIMALS
    It is dangerous for you and can also cause problems for the animal. Also, of course, never feed the animals.
  6. ATTENTION WITH MEMORIES
    Be sure to carefully evaluate what you bring home from your wildlife adventure.

Mammals

Lesser Grison

[Galictis cuja]

They have a long, slender body, short legs, and a bushy tail. They have a long neck and a small head with a flattened forehead and rounded ears. With a head-body length of 27 to 52 cm and a 14 to 19 cm long tail. Adults weigh between 1.2 and 2.4 kg.

The top of the head, back and flanks, and tail have thick black protective hairs with beige tips on a softer undercoat, giving them a grayish-gray color. The rest of the body is black or almost black, except for a pale beige stripe running from forehead to shoulders along the lower margin of the gray hair area. The feet are webbed, with five toes ending in sharp, curved claws.

Females have a gestation period of 55 days and give birth between 1 and 10 young, which feed on mother's milk for approximately 10 weeks.

Guanaco

Like the llama, the guanaco is a camelid that is even rarer than the alpaca. It is covered by a thick, double coat that protects it.

Cougar

[Puma concolor]

Until the end of the 20th century, 32 puma subspecies had been recorded, however, a genetic study of mitochondrial DNA showed that many of them are too similar to be recognized as different taxa.

Cougars are slender and agile cats. The adult standing size is around 70 to 90 cm in height at the shoulders. The length of adult males is around 2.7 m long from nose to tip of tail, males have an average weight of between 60 to 100 kg. The cougar can be as big as the jaguar, but less robust since the cougar is slender.

The coloration of the puma is uniform (hence the Latin name concolor), the coat is generally golden, but can be silver-gray or reddish in color, with slight patches on the body, including near the jaws, chin and the neck. Cougars have big legs; proportionally the largest hind legs in the feline family and strong and developed shoulders.

Zorrillo

[Conepatus humboldtii]

Its main characteristic feature is the strong and fetid odor that its olfactory glands secrete. This scent is emitted when the skunk feels threatened. Before emanation, the skunk adopts a different body position depending on the species

The coat is basically bicolor, presenting mostly a design with a black background color on which there are white stripes or bands, which suggests that, like other species with similar patterns, they warn their aggressors of the possession of some kind of hidden defense. They are omnivores, they can feed on insects, eggs, fruits, small mammals, birds, and honey.

Andean Fox

[Lycalopex culpaeus]

It was described by Molina in 1782 as Canis culpaeus. Later it was transferred to the genus Lycalopex. In the mid-2010s it could be found spelled as Pseudalopex culpaeus, synonymous with the accepted name.

Both the legs and the head are reddish; the belly, neck and mouth, white; and the back, gray streaked with black. The tail is heavily covered with gray hairs, which turn black towards the tip. Specimens from the South American continental zone weigh between 7 to 9 kg, but the Tierra del Fuego subspecies (L. c. Lycoides) is significantly higher, reaching 14 kg.

Birds

Condor

[Vultur gryphus]

It is a large black bird, with white feathers around the neck and on parts of the wings. The head lacks feathers and is red in color, and can change color according to the emotional state of the bird. Unlike most birds of prey, the male is larger than the female.

Adults can measure up to 142 cm in length, 1 m in height, and 270 to 330 cm in wingspan, and males weigh from 11 to 15 kg and females from 8 to 11 kg. They have a bare head, generally reddish in color, although it can change according to the mood of the animal; very sharp edge and hooked end. The wings are long and wide, and the legs, not prehensile, have short and slightly curved nails, and with the raised rear toe insertion.

It is also one of the birds that flies to the highest altitudes, the Andean condor can fly using the vertical upward thermal currents of warm air and can reach up to 6500 meters above sea level and then it can glide for hundreds of kilometers almost without moving its outstretched wings. , the condor consumes very little energy and thanks to its dense plumage it can withstand freezing climates.

Peregrine Falcon

[Falco peregrinus]

It is a large hawk, the size of a raven, with a bluish-gray back and a whitish underside with dark spots; the head is black and has a broad and characteristic mustache, also black in color. It does not normally fly at speeds above 100 km / h, but when diving or when hunting and making a dive attack it can reach more than 300 km / h, making it the fastest animal in the world.

Its body length varies between 34 and 58 cm, and its wingspan is between 80 and 120 cm. Its thin, pointed wings give it a good boost. Males weigh between 440 and 750 g, while notably older females weigh between 910 and 1500 g.

Pigeon

[Columbidae]

They are birds endowed with great intelligence (if compared with other birds), although already among other less intelligent birds an important intelligence has been discovered that has even allowed an interaction between Homo sapiens and birds, despite the fact that such smart interaction.

The nests, normally weak, are made of small branches, and the eggs, usually two, are incubated by both sexes. Pigeons naturally feed on seeds and fruits.

Aside from having a great sense of direction, the pigeon is one of the fastest flying birds: it reaches 56 km / h. Pigeons are also characterized by their keen sense of sight.

Scissor-tailed

[Tyrannus savana]

It is a remarkable bird for its very long tail made up of six pairs of feathers, the longest being the lateral ones, which are what give it its characteristic shape. The plumage of the head and back is black, while that of the throat, chest and belly is silvery white. The feathers of the head, in their lower part, where they are inserted into the skin, have a yellow coloration that can only be seen when it bristles them, which does not happen frequently.

Poses in visible places such as fences, branches or thistles, observes the insects and goes out to capture them. It is very aggressive towards other species and even larger birds, such as caranchos and chimpanzees, which it persecutes and attacks insistently.

Reptiles/Amphibians

Austral Cascabel

[Crotalus durissus terrificus]

It shows a solid, robust body, laterally compressed and ventrally flattened. It can grow to a maximum length of around 180 cm. Every time an individual sheds their skin, a new segment is formed in the caudal appendix, and this may also become detached in part due to some eventuality.

The head is large, sub-triangular in shape, separated from the rest of the body by a narrow neck. The body is heavily scaled, also presenting accentuated protrusions or tuberculations. These forms are somewhat smoothed out reaching the hind limb.

Argentine Coral

[Micrurus pyrrhocryptus]

It is a kind of ophidian with powerful poison, stealthy habits, but with a powerful poison capable of killing a grown man. But it does not have the aggressiveness of other poisonous snakes, so accidents are very rare.

Its coloration presents a general tricolor pattern. It shows very wide full red rings and other black ones even wider. Each of the latter presents two narrow white rings, which are positioned away from the central area of the black portion, giving a wider black sector between the white rings than between each of these and the red sectors.

Gecko Salamanca

[Homonota fasciata]

It is a nocturnal saurian with an insectivorous diet that lives in south-central South America.

It is a medium-sized lizard, with a robust conformation, with a length of about 60 mm between snout and vent, reaching a total length of 140 mm. Its dorsal chromatic pattern, although highly variable due to the action of the chromatophores, consisting of large quadrangular dark brown or blackish spots, which are separated by thin whitish spaces. The color of the belly is immaculate white. In addition, it does not have an internal scale, it shows vertically rhomboidal and transversely rounded subcaudal scales, and the ear canal has toothed edges formed by conical scales.

It is an adaptable lizard, terrestrial and nocturnal. Its versatility allows it to survive both in forested subtropical environments and in desert shrub stations. He is well known by the common public with whom he lives as he usually lives inside humble human dwellings.

Painted Turtle

[Trachemys scripta]

Lives in swamps and other fresh waters. It feeds on various animals and plants.

These subspecies can reach 20 to 30 cm in length, although they rarely exceed 25 cm. Males are smaller but have longer nails and tails. Females are larger than males, however they have much shorter tails and nails.

It has been introduced, due to its popularity as a pet, which is why it is considered an invasive species.

Argentine Land Turtle

[Chelonoidis chilensis]

It is distributed from the Gran Chaco to the north of Patagonia. It is a fundamentally herbivorous species, as it feeds on the stems and fruits of cacti, grasses, herbaceous plants and legume pods. Also, from leaves of plants, grasses, fruits, vegetables and tubers; rarely supplements its diet with some invertebrates, such as snails, slugs, and worms.

Females are 32 cm long and males 26 cm. Freiberg describes a giant specimen of the following dimensions: Carapace: length 433 mm; width 365 mm (both in a straight line); height: 180 mm; Plastron: length: 295 mm; width 266 mm.

Insects

Camoati

[Polybia occidentalis]

They are eusocial wasps that form numerous swarms whose nests hang mostly on trees, but also on rocks as well as on the roofs of houses and sheds. It feeds on nectar, insects, and carbohydrate sources, while preying on birds; ants prey on them and parasitize them. The P. occidentalis worker wasps sting each other to communicate that it is time to go to work.

They are distinguished by their black and yellow colors. The base color is black and they have a few yellow stripes on the abdomen. The wings are thin and the thorax is attached to the abdomen by a long and thin petiole. The workers have an average dry weight of 3.80 mg to 6.71 mg. The length of the costal margin of the wings is 3.6 to 4.7 mm. The queen wasp differs from the workers because they have larger ovaries.

Argentine Red Ant

[Solenopsis invicta]

The red fire ant is more aggressive than most native ant species and has a painful sting. Ants respond to pheromones that are released by the first ant to attack. They then bite at the same time, often killing small animals by overloading their immune systems.

They are very hardy ants and have adapted to survive both floods and drought conditions. If the ants perceive a rise in the water level in their colonies, they will coalesce to form a huge ball or raft that is capable of floating on the water, with the workers on the outside and the queen on the inside.

House Fly

[Musca domestica]

It is the most common and common fly in most of the Earth's climates.

Adults can measure about 5-8 mm in length and 13 to 15 mm in wingspan. Its thorax is gray, with four dark longitudinal lines on the back, the lower part of the abdomen is yellow or orange, occasionally transparent on the sides and with a dark central band that widens and covers the last abdominal segments.

They can be carriers of infectious-contagious diseases that they can transmit when consuming human food, which they contaminate when they are later ingested by humans.

Common Mosquito

[Culex sp.]

Its behavior makes it habitually enter houses and it will almost always attack us between sunset and sunrise due to its nocturnal habits.

The larvae are aquatic like those of other mosquitoes, and they breed in all kinds of containers, channels, and structures with water, which means that we find them everywhere. In addition, we will find them both in clean and polluted waters.

In its larval stage it feeds on organic material and plants, while in the pupal stage they do not feed, preparing for the final phase of its cycle. When they reach maturity, females seek blood from animals (including humans) to reproduce, and males feed on pollen, fruit juice, and nectar.

Vinchuca

[Triatoma infestans]

The vinchuca presents different forms according to the growth stage in which it is, with a size that evolves from 2 mm to 2 cm or 3 cm in the adult state. It is brown in color, with a border of transverse bands that alternate in brown and light colors. The head is sharp with two slight protrusions that are the eyes; it has, like all insects, six legs and a pair of antennae. Its body is flat, but when it feeds its abdomen swells and its wings are raised, which are normally folded.

One of the characteristics of this insect is that it does not deposit the parasite in human tissue, but rather that, when it bites a person and sucks the blood, generally from areas close to the mouth such as the lips, their intestine swells and It forces it to defecate, depositing parasites on its victim's skin. Because of the itching, people scratch, and it is they themselves that incorporate the parasite into their tissues through this action.

Fishes

Carpa

[Cyprinus carpio]

It is native to stagnant or slow water bodies in temperate regions. It is a ubiquitous animal, easy to grow and has the characteristic of being ectothermal and eurythermal. It is a very resistant animal, capable of living in brackish waters with a temperature between 17 and 24 Â° C.

The common carp can measure 1.2 m in length and weigh up to 40 kg, although normally, in the adult state, it measures 60 to 90 cm, and its weight is around 9 kg. Nothing forming schools; it is omnivorous, and resistant to a wide variety of climatic conditions. There have been cases of specimens that have lived to 65 years.

Toothy

[Oligosarcus jenynsii]

Males can reach 31 cm in total length and 330 g in weight.

Eat fish, macrocrustaceans and aquatic insects.

Streams, rivers and lagoons of Argentina, Uruguay and Rio Grande do Sul (Brazil).

Morenita

[Jenynsia lineata]

Like other species of the genus Jenynsia, it is characterized by presenting a tubular gonopodium formed by the 3rd, 6th and 7th anal rays - which is associated with a spermatic duct - and for having the adult specimens tricuspid occlusion teeth in the mandible . The body color is light greenish-gray; on the sides it shows 6 to 8 dark thin lines, or dotted longitudinal stripes. The fins are colorless. Both sexes have the same coloration. The unfertilized female has an orange spot to the right or left of the anal fin. The male is considerably thinner and smaller than the female, who measures 6.6 cm.

Of all the species of the genus Jenynsia, J. lineata is the one with the largest distribution, widely covering the east-central and south-central South America. It inhabits both fresh and brackish water biotopes from temperate-cold to temperate-warm climates. On some occasions, this fish has been found living in seawater, along the coastline and in tide pools between the rocks.

Pejerrey

[Odontesthes bonariensis]

It is a fast fish, fusiform body, slightly compressed, it has a silver color with two longitudinal darker bands, a marked ventral curve, a strong head, bony on the upper part, a protractile mouth; two fins on the back (a first small with flexible rays, the second longer and flexible radius), ventral fins without serrations or spines. Silver coloration and somewhat bluish iridescence and a bright stripe on the flank. The tail is shaped like a fork. It lives mostly in schools.

Fast growing, it is one of the largest in adulthood; some specimens have reached 60 cm. long and 20 cm thick, and 3 kg in weight, however, the average size is smaller, 40 cm at 800 grams. Its high reproduction rate and the quality of its meat position it as an important species for intensive and extensive crops.

Rainbow Trout

[Oncorhynchus mykiss]

The maximum length recorded was a specimen of 120 cm, although the normal length is 60 cm. It has three to four spines on the dorsal fin. Breeding males do not show changes in the morphology of the head or the rest of the body as striking as those of other species of the same genus; the coloration of the skin varies greatly with the habitat and size; those that reside in rivers permanently are darker, similar to anadromous in reproductive age, while those that reside in lakes are lighter in color.

It is a fish from subtropical to cold waters and anadromous, that lives in the sea near the bottom in a depth range between 0 and 200 m, going up rivers to spawn.
