Useful information

    Advice on behavior towards wildlife:

  1. IF POSSIBLE, VISIT THE ANIMALS IN THEIR NATURAL HABITAT
    If you plan the most comfortable option of visiting a sanctuary or zoo, make sure it is accredited and professionally managed.
  2. SEE ANIMALS BEHAVE NATURALLY
    Snake charmers and monkey circuses may be alluring, but what happens behind the scenes can horrify you. To avoid encouraging these practices, find a respected elephant sanctuary or take a jeep ride to admire the elephants in their natural habitat.
  3. IT IS PROHIBITED TO CARRY
    This instruction might disappoint you, but you have to understand the mistreatment that surrounds facilities that offer experiences of active participation or close contact with animals.
  4. SPEND MONEY IN A SUSTAINABLE WAY
    If you are really concerned about animal welfare and wildlife sustainability, consider hiring an expedition group rather than organizing each activity yourself.
  5. RESPECT ANIMALS
    It is dangerous for you and can also cause problems for the animal. Also, of course, never feed the animals.
  6. ATTENTION WITH MEMORIES
    Be sure to carefully evaluate what you bring home from your wildlife adventure.

Presentation

At a time when different animal species gain ground due to the health emergency and social, preventive and compulsory isolation, and therefore contact between humans and wild animals will become more common once society returns to its normal routine. For this reason, this page aims to develop protection strategies based on raising awareness about the importance of some species in our province. This information will then be used as an indicator of environmental quality management.

For this purpose, we will seek to create a reliable source of information which allows the user to learn about the local fauna, in this way he will know what he is facing and how to proceed to avoid any conflict that may cause a danger to himself as well as to the animal. in question.

On this page you can also find information about associations and NGOs related to wildlife, which not only can provide more information, but can also provide support in extreme cases in which specialized help is needed to deal with wildlife.

Introduction

The province of Mendoza has a great variety of mammals. Among the carnivores we can mention the fox -both gray and red-, skunks, ferrets, pumas and weasels, it also has a great variety of carnivorous and poultry mammals that take refuge in caves during the day, especially pumas or mountain lions. There are poultry specimens that take refuge during the day in caves, such as the pichiciego from Mendoza. In the Andean district you can see condors and in the sub-Andean birds of prey: eagles and hawks, to which are added rodents such as mice and guinea pigs, owls, red chest, pititorras and different types of snakes and insects. Towards the south you can see skunks, vizcachas, guanacos, partridges, rheas, and birds such as cardinals and parrots. Aquatic birds also inhabit the region, such as the black-necked swan, heron and flamingos. Likewise, the ñandú and ñandú petiso, which is a species of land bird, typical of the mountainous area.

High mountain

This region, located above 3,000 meters above sea level, and up to 4,500 meters, has a very scarce fauna, conditioned by the lack of food, extreme temperatures and the presence of snow and wind storms. Among the birds, the condor stands out. Mammals are represented by guanacos and pumas.

Mountain

In the lands located at heights less than 3,000 meters there are numerous valleys and streams where the fauna finds better shelter and more food than in the high peaks. Among the characteristic birds of the area are the short Andean rhea, the small mountain partridge, the current-cut duck, the crested duck, the condor, the black eagle and the harrier, the kestrel, the Virginian owl.

The most common mammals in the area are: guanacos, pumas, red foxes, guinea pigs, mice, European hares and two species of bat, the common and the long-eared bat. There are also reptiles, such as the spiny-tailed lizard, the yarará ñata, and the rat snake.

Preserrana Zone

This area has a fauna similar to that of the mountain, but damaged by the action of man. There are, among the birds, the common rhea, the moorish eagle, the harrier, the falcon and the chimango. The characteristic mammals are the cougar, the gray fox, the wild cat, the skunk.

The mountain

The mountain includes the extensive plains of the east of Mendoza, in part very medanos, with radicals dotted with groves of carob and chañar. Here live the ñandú, the partridge, the small partridge, the martineta, the chimango, the carancho, the harrier and the kestrel. There are also mammals, such as bats, furry, gray foxes, and bobcats. Reptiles include lizards, green lizards, vizcachera boas, and tortoises.

Payunia

In this arid landscape, shaped by volcanic activity with vegetation adapted to the lack of water, the characteristic species are the following: short rheas or choique, black-headed jote, chimango, moorish eagle, harrier, martineta, big-headed plover, chingolo and calandria . There are mammals such as marmots, Patagonian piches, European hares and rabbits, maras, vizcachas, chinchillones, guinea pigs, tunduques, guanacos, pumas, Patagonian or chiñes skunks and gray foxes. Among the reptiles are the nata yarará, the rat snake, the matuasto and the lizard. The most common amphibians are the Andean toad and frog.

Ambientes Acuáticos

This region, which includes swamps, estuaries and natural lagoons, such as Llancanelo lagoon, Blanca lagoon, Los Álamos lagoon, La salinilla, San Miguel reservoirs, Atuel and salado estuaries, has a wide avifauna, represented by flamingos, coscorobas swans, black-necked swans, silver macaws, witch herons, black herons, coots, gray-crowned ducks, grebe ducks, capuchin ducks, common and royal teros. Among the mammals is the nutria or nutria.
